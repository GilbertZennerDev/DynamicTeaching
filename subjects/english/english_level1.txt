**1. The Simple 3-Word Sentence:** Choose one of the following verbs: *run, eat, play, see*. Write a grammatically correct and complete sentence containing **exactly three words** using that verb (e.g., *I run fast* is too long). Repeat this exercise for each of the four verbs.
**=END=**

**2. Plural Power:** List the **singular** and **plural** forms for the following five nouns: *child, mouse, foot, woman, tomato*.
**=END=**

**3. Present Continuous Snapshots:** Look out a window or imagine a simple scene. Write three sentences describing what is happening **right now**, using the **Present Continuous** tense (e.g., *The cat is sleeping.*).
**=END=**

**4. The 'Be' Swap:** Write the following three sentences, correcting the form of the verb **"to be"** for the given subject:
* She **are** happy.
* They **is** at home.
* I **am** a teacher yesterday. (Change *am* and *yesterday* to match)
**=END=**

**5. Adjective Placement:** Write a single sentence using each of the following adjectives to describe a noun: *big, yellow, quiet*. Place the adjective **before** the noun it describes.
**=END=**

**6. Asking 'Yes/No':** Take the following three statements and turn each one into a **Yes/No question**:
* You like pizza.
* She can swim.
* They went to the park.
**=END=**

**7. Preposition Puzzle:** Fill in the correct simple preposition (*in, on, under, at*) for the blank spaces:
* The book is \_\_\_\_\_ the table.
* I wake up \_\_\_\_\_ 7 o'clock.
* We live \_\_\_\_\_ London.
**=END=**

**8. 'Do' vs. 'Does':** Write two different simple questions using the auxiliary verb **'do'** and two different simple questions using the auxiliary verb **'does'**.
**=END=**

**9. Possessive 'S':** Write the following three phrases using the correct **possessive 's'**:
* The car of John.
* The toys of the children.
* The book of my mother.
**=END=**

**10. Simple Past Action:** Describe your breakfast this morning in three short sentences, using only the **Simple Past** tense (e.g., *I ate cereal. It was good.*).
**=END=**
